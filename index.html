How To Perform Nav Durga Sadhana

Evening Japa

Every evening, we awaken one form of Devi by chanting Her mantras. Chant the preliminary mantras followed by the main mantra of the day. In-app prompts will guide you at every step.

Morning Yagya

Every morning, we complete our invocation of the Devi by performing a Yagna with Her mantras. Perform the preliminary steps followed by the main mantra offerings of the day. In-app prompts will guide you every step of the way.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Where do I find the Nav Durga Sadhana on the app?

Tap on the walking lion on the home screen.

2. How do I perform the Sadhana?

Click on the lion and follow the in-app prompts. The Sadhana has two parts - a Japa in the evening and a Yagna the next morning.

3. What if I miss a day of the Sadhana?

If you miss a day, your Sadhana is compromised. If, however, you are unable to perform the Yagna on a particular day, you may repeat that day’s Japa and continue your Sadhana.

4. Is there an age limit for the Sadhana?

Anyone over the age of 12 is welcome to perform the Sadhana (including those on their menstrual cycle).

5. Why must I offer Dakshina?

In Vedic tradition, Dakshina is a devotee’s expression of gratitude to the Universe for the opportunity to perform a Sadhana. Your contribution also helps us maintain the digital world of the Sadhana App.

6. Is digital worship the same as physical worship?

100%. Mental worship, in fact, deepens our focus and devotion, helping us forge an unshakeable bond with the Divine.
